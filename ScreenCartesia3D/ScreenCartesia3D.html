Cartesia 3D
Bi-polar directional programming
case in which troublesome dysarthria was overcome with directional steering
Overview
  • A 56-year-old right-handed male diagnosed with Parkinson’s disease approved for DBS surgery
  • Motor fluctuations observed with good preoperative levodopa response
  • UPDRS III score improved 38 points (62 to 24) in meds-on state
Methods
  • Lead placement confirmed by postoperative CT merged with preoperative MRI
  • A detailed monopolar review was assessing dysarthria, bradykinesia, and rigidity
  • During monopolar review, patient experienced severe stimulation-induced dysarthria from lead in left hemisphere at all contacts postoperatively and at 6-month review
